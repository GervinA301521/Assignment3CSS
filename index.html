About Skittles

Skittles are a popular fruit-flavored candy produced by the Wrigley Company. They were first introduced in 1974 and are known for their vibrant colors and fruity taste. Each pack of Skittles contains a rainbow of flavors, including strawberry, lemon, green apple, orange, and grape.

Interesting Facts

  • Skittles were originally created in the UK and made their way to the United States in 1979.
  • The original flavor in the United States was lime, but it was later replaced with green apple.
  • Skittles are gluten-free and suitable for vegetarians.
  • The candy shell on Skittles is what gives them their iconic crunch.
